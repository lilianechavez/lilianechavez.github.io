The Vlogbrothers

A Mini Biography

The vlogbrothers are also known as the famous Youtubers, Hank and John Green. They are known as one of the key founders of the style creators on the platform make content for their audiences. They started making videos in 2007 for a personal project they decided to undertake; and soon became a hit within just a few months.


Brotherhood 2.0

When Hank and John first started making videos, they called it Brotherhood 2.0. They goal was to only contact each other through videos on YouTube, and to post videos everyday for a year. They came up with rules about the project, and if either broke one of these rules, then their auidence would get to decide their punishment. Hank and John would talk about their day, record themselves doing silly things, and explain any important, complicated topic about the world. Sometimes they would mix both silly activities and serious topics, such John's infamous video discussing the civil war in Sri Lanka while putting peanut butter on his face.

The Road to Fame

At first, the vlogbrtohers would only receive about a hundred views for each video. However, since the borthers proudly proclaim themselves as nerds, Hank green decided to write a song about Harry Potter and post it. The video was a success as it appeared on the trending list on YouTube's home page. Soon afterwards, they began receiving thousands of views for each video (which was a lot at the time).

After Brotherhood 2.0

Once the two brothers realized they had a large audience that enjoyed their videos, they decided to continue uploading content, only this time they decided to each post a video once a week. They slowly gained more subscribers and captivated their audience with their informative yet quirky videos. Many of their most popular videos deal with heavy, but important topics such as the chlorea outbreak in Yemen, the economic crisis in Greece, the refugee crisis in Syria, and many more.

  • The Project for Awesome

    Hank and John Green decided to use their fame to their advantage to donate to charity. They ran a fundraiser through their videos in December. They were able to raise several thousand dollars; and as a result, they distributed the money among several charitable organizations. After several years, the Green brothers started The Foundation to Decrease World Suck as a registered charity. Every year since the first fundraiser, in December the brothers do a 48 hour livestream called the Project for Awesome asking the YouTube community to donate and to post videos explaining which charities should receive the smaller $5,000 grants. They also ask the public to vote which organizations should receive the largest grants.

  • Complexly

    In 2011 YouTube decided to sponsor and fund several educational channels as part of a program. This lead to the creation of their educational channels, Crash Course and SciShow. The purpose of the channels are to provide short lessons of common courses taken in high school and college and to inform the public about the latest new in science. After the channels' success, the Green brothers launched several other educational channels about health and healthcare, financing, art, sex education, and much more. These channels soon became a part of their prodution company called Complexly. The companny's goal is to educate the public about various topics so we can imagine the world more complexly.

  • VidCon

    Once YouTube became increasingly popular, Hank and John Green started the first ever large-scale video conference in 2010, also known as Vidcon. They invited many famous creators to do panels and Q and A's. Even though it was largely successful, YouTube did not become its official sponsor until 2013.
